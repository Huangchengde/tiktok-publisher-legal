TikTok Publisher Helper

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 11, 2026

1. Information processed

This workflow may process videos, audio, captions, hashtags, publication settings, TikTok account identifiers returned by TikTok, access tokens, refresh tokens, upload status, and logs needed to complete creator-approved publishing tasks.

2. How information is used

Information is used to edit videos, prepare TikTok-ready media, request creator approval, upload approved content to TikTok, monitor upload or publish status, troubleshoot failures, and maintain basic audit records.

3. TikTok authorization data

TikTok access tokens and refresh tokens, when used, are handled as confidential credentials. They are used only to call TikTok APIs for the authorized account and requested scopes. Tokens should be stored server-side and should not be exposed in public pages, client-side code, screenshots, or shared logs.

4. Sharing

Content and metadata may be sent to TikTok when the creator approves an upload or publication action. Information is not sold. Information may be disclosed if required by law, to prevent abuse, or to protect the operator's rights and systems.

5. Retention

Local working files, rendered videos, upload logs, and publishing records may be retained only as long as needed for editing, publishing, troubleshooting, audit, or legal compliance. Users may request deletion of locally retained media and related workflow records where deletion is technically and legally feasible.

6. Security

Reasonable technical and organizational measures should be used to protect tokens, media files, and publishing records. No method of transmission or storage is fully secure, so users should avoid submitting unnecessary sensitive information.

7. User control

Users may revoke TikTok authorization through TikTok's account or developer authorization controls. Revoking access may prevent future uploads or publishing actions through this workflow.

8. Contact

For privacy questions or deletion requests, contact the operator through the GitHub repository that hosts this site or through the private communication channel where this workflow is operated.
